## Five Distribution Strategies

### Strategy 1: Micro-Site Network

**Goal**: Own the content that leads to your product.

**How it works**:
1. Identify search queries your target users make BEFORE they need your product
2. Acquire expired domains in those topics
3. Restore/create content answering those queries
4. Naturally lead readers to your product as the solution

**Example** — You built a budgeting app:

```
Domains to acquire:
├── savemoneyguide.com      → "How to save money" content
├── budgetingtips.org       → "Budgeting for beginners" guide
├── personalfinance101.com  → Financial literacy hub
├── frugalliving.net        → Lifestyle content
└── debtfreejourney.com     → Debt payoff stories

Each site:
├── 10-20 SEO articles
├── Email capture
├── "Tools I use" page featuring YOUR app
└── Contextual links to your app throughout
```

**Traffic flow**:
```
Google: "how to budget money"
  → budgetingtips.org article ranks
  → Reader learns budgeting
  → CTA: "Track your budget with [YourApp]"
  → Click → Your app
```

**You own the entire funnel.**

---

### Strategy 2: Keyword Domain Arbitrage

**Goal**: Rank instantly for high-intent keywords.

**How it works**:
1. Find exact-match or partial-match expired domains for your key search terms
2. Build minimal landing pages optimized for those keywords
3. Domain age + keyword match = faster ranking
4. Capture traffic searching for exactly what you offer

**Example** — You built a time tracking tool:

```
Expired domains to target:
├── besttimetracker.com     → "best time tracker" (2.4K searches/mo)
├── freetimetracking.io     → "free time tracking" (1.8K searches/mo)
├── timetrackingapp.com     → "time tracking app" (3.1K searches/mo)
├── employeetimetracker.net → "employee time tracker" (1.2K searches/mo)
└── projecttimetracking.org → "project time tracking" (900 searches/mo)

Each domain:
├── Single landing page
├── Comparison table (you vs competitors)
├── Direct CTA to your main app
└── Maybe capture email for nurture
```

**Why this works**:
- Exact-match domains still carry SEO weight
- Aged domains bypass Google sandbox
- High-intent traffic (they're actively searching)
- You control the narrative (your app = #1)

---

### Strategy 3: Authority Redirect Network

**Goal**: Transfer link equity to boost your main domain.

**How it works**:
1. Acquire expired domains with strong backlink profiles (DA 20+)
2. 301 redirect entire domain to your main site
3. Link equity flows to your domain
4. Your main domain climbs in authority

**Example** — Your main app is at myapp.com (DA 15):

```
Acquired expired domains:
├── oldtechblog.com    (DA 35, 200 backlinks) → 301 → myapp.com
├── defunctstartup.io  (DA 28, 150 backlinks) → 301 → myapp.com/features
├── shutdownsaas.com   (DA 22, 80 backlinks)  → 301 → myapp.com/pricing
└── abandonedblog.net  (DA 25, 120 backlinks) → 301 → myapp.com/blog

Result:
├── myapp.com inherits ~550 backlinks
├── Domain authority increases over 3-6 months
├── Existing pages rank higher
└── New content indexes faster
```

**Caution**: 
- Only redirect relevant niches (tech blog → tech app = good)
- Avoid spammy backlink profiles
- Don't redirect too many at once (looks unnatural)

### Strategy 4: Audience Pre-Building

**Goal**: Build an audience BEFORE you launch.

**How it works**:
1. Acquire expired domain in your target niche
2. Restore as content site / newsletter
3. Build email list and community
4. Launch your product to warm audience

**Example** — You're building a design tool (not launched yet):

```
6 months before launch:
├── Acquire: designresources.com (expired, had 50K monthly visitors)
├── Restore: Curated design resources, tutorials, freebies
├── Build: Email list grows to 15,000 designers
├── Engage: Weekly newsletter, community Discord

Launch day:
├── Email 15,000 subscribers about your new tool
├── 3,000 click through
├── 500 sign up day one
├── You have customers before competitors know you exist
```

**This is how smart founders "launch to an audience."** They didn't build the audience from scratch — they acquired it.

---

### Strategy 5: Comparison & Review Sites

**Goal**: Control the narrative when users compare options.

**How it works**:
1. Acquire domains matching "[product] vs [competitor]" or "[category] reviews"
2. Create comparison content with your product prominently featured
3. Rank for high-intent comparison searches
4. Users "discover" your product while researching

**Example** — You built a Notion alternative:

```
Domains to acquire:
├── notionalternatives.com    → "Top 10 Notion alternatives" (you're #1)
├── bestnotiontools.com       → Tools that work with Notion + yours
├── productivityappreviews.io → Review site (favorable to you)
├── notionvs[yourapp].com     → Direct comparison (you win)
└── workspacetools.org        → Broader category (you're featured)

Content approach:
├── Genuinely useful comparisons
├── Honest pros/cons (builds trust)
├── Your product solves specific pain points competitors don't
├── CTA: "Try [YourApp] free for 14 days"
```

**This is what big companies pay PR firms millions to do.** You're doing it with $50 domains.

## Domain Selection for Distribution

### Scoring Criteria (Distribution Focus)

Different from flip scoring. We prioritize distribution value:

| Factor | Points | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Keyword relevance** | 30 | Does domain match your niche? |
| **Existing backlinks** | 25 | More links = more authority transfer |
| **Domain age** | 15 | Older = more trust, faster ranking |
| **Wayback content** | 15 | Restorable content = faster launch |
| **TLD quality** | 10 | .com/.org/.io preferred |
| **Brandability** | 5 | Memorable helps direct traffic |

### Domain Categories by Use Case

| Category | Characteristics | Use For |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Authority Sites** | DA 25+, 100+ backlinks | Redirect juice or content hub |
| **Keyword Exact Match** | Contains target keyword | Landing pages, SEO arbitrage |
| **Niche Content Sites** | Wayback has 50+ pages | Restore as micro-site |
| **Audience Sites** | Had email list or community | Pre-built audience capture |
| **Competitor Adjacent** | Related to competitor brand | Comparison content |

### Red Flags for Distribution Domains

Avoid domains that will hurt more than help:

- ❌ Spammy backlink profile (check Ahrefs/Moz)
- ❌ Previous Google penalty (manual action)
- ❌ Adult/gambling history (unless that's your niche)
- ❌ Trademark conflicts (legal risk)
- ❌ Completely unrelated niche (confuses Google)

```
    │ Authority Hub │ │ Keyword Sites │ │ Content Sites │
    │ guide.com     │ │ bestapp.io    │ │ tips.org      │
    └───────┬───────┘ └───────┬───────┘ └───────┬───────┘
            │                │                │
            └────────────────┴────────────────┘
                             │
                    Cross-link between sites
                    (contextual, natural)
```

**Linking rules:**
- Every site links to main product (contextual, not spammy)
- Content sites link to each other (topical relevance)
- Keyword sites link to content sites (supporting content)
- Never more than 2-3 outbound links per page
- Anchor text varies (brand, keyword, generic)
